FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Where can I buy boltcards?

What you need are blank NXP NTAG424DNA cards, you can buy them from many different vendors. Coin Corner sell more expensive ones printed with cool Bitcoin cartoons on.

  • CoinCorner.com
  • ZipNFC.com
  • ShopNFC.com
  • Identiv.com

How do I set up my Boltcard?

First head to the git link at the bottom of this page and set up your boltcard service as described in the boltcard repository

Order some blank NFC cards online and then download and install the NFC programming app to set up your card

How does bolt card work?

Each NFC card is programmed with your boltcard service's lnurlw:// address and some secure keys that generate a new unique hash each time the card is scanned. This means that the card outputs this LNURL and unique numbers to the merchant.

The merchant can then use these values to make a one time call to your bolt card servi/ce, after that the card must be tapped again to get fresh values.

The bolt card service then checks the payment value against your payment rules (currently a per tx and per day limits), but in the future this could be whitelisted merchants or any other kind of rules

What does a merchant need to do to accept Boltcards?

The merchant just needs to use an android device that supports one of the merchant apps or systems mentioned in the about section above.

Why accept/use a Boltcards?

There are many barriers to entry for merchants and users to start accepting bitcoin. Scanning QR codes while pretty quick and compatible gives a bit of a clunky user experience for small payments.

Tapping your Boltcard provides a user experience that matches the big payment providers

Not least to mention that the fees for using the Bitcoin Lightning network are now at least a magnitude smaller than the legacy payment providers

TLDR: Cost of business savings for the merchant and the customer!
